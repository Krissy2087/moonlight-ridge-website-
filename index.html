The Setting

One of America's great wild places.
Your private perch above it all.

Red River Gorge has been carving through the Daniel Boone National Forest for 70 million years. A hundred natural arches. Ancient sandstone cliffs. Waterfalls that appear after rain. A forest that turns fire in October. World-class climbing. Trails that lead somewhere worth going.

Moonlight Ridge sits above it on 23 private acres. The ridgeline stretched out below you. The forest on every side. No neighbors in sight. No noise except what the gorge makes on its own.

This is where you come when you're ready to actually stop.

The Gorge

The gorge is out your door.
Here's where to start.

Red River Gorge packs more into a weekend than most destinations offer in a week. Moonlight Ridge puts you at the center of all of it.

Hiking

Natural Bridge + Sky Bridge

Two of Kentucky's most iconic natural arches, both within 15 minutes. Natural Bridge State Resort Park has trails for every level. Sky Bridge is a 5-minute walk and worth every step.

Climbing

World-Class Rock Climbing

RRG is one of the top sport climbing destinations east of the Rockies. Muir Valley, Pendergrass-Murray, and dozens of crags are minutes away. Stop at Miguel's Pizza — it's an institution.

Fall Foliage

October in the Gorge

Red River Gorge turns spectacular in October — some of the best fall color in the eastern US. Book early. This is peak season for a reason, and Moonlight Ridge fills up first.

Food + Culture

Miguel's Pizza + Local Favorites

Miguel's Pizza has fed climbers and hikers since 1984. It's a rite of passage. The Natural Bridge State Resort Park also has a full restaurant for easier evenings.

Stargazing

Dark Skies Above the Ridge

Far enough from city light to matter. The mountaintop location and open ridgeline make Moonlight Ridge one of the best stargazing spots in the gorge area. The hot tub helps.

All Seasons

The Gorge in Every Season

Spring brings waterfalls and wildflowers. Summer is for long trails and late evenings. Fall is peak. Winter is underrated — snow on the ridge, steam from the hot tub, no crowds.
